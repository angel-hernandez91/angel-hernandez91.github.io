Nibbler and Fry

About Nibbler

My name is Dr. Nibbles Nibbler Nibblstein. I hold PhD's in Neurobiology, Computer Science, Physics, Mathematics, and Chicano Studies. Several decades as go I wrote the infamous 1973 paper the Sex of a Fly, which brought the Life Sciences into the mainstream. But not all that glittered was gold; due to allegations of falsified data I was exiled from the scientific community and my work was lost to the sands of time.

Many people believed that I falsified data to rise to the top of my field so I could live a life of luxury. A life of golden showers, fast cars, and even faster, hairless men. I was constantly harassed by the press, and my closest friends and lover, Carlito, abandoned me. It's been a wild ride. I developed a severe addiction to cocaine, robbed several liquor stores, and ended up in jail where I thought I would waste away until Angel broke me out by paying off the guards.

Academic research is a thing of the past for me. These days I spend my time napping, being tormented by that orange bastard Fry, rubbing my face on Angel's beard, and trying to get as many belly pats as possible. I never spoke out back then because I was afraid I would look like a lunatic. But now, in the days of Russian hacking, crooked politicians, and a rejected Cheeto for a President, this doesn't seem so crazy after all.

I was set up to fail by Big Oil because I discovered a way to turn Mexican tears into fuel using bromoacetone, quarks, and fruit fly penises using an Advanced Quantum Deep Learning AI Model. This would've completely disolved our dependence on oil, and ushered in an era where Mexicans were venerated, respected, and accepted into this country. It was too radical for the time, and possibly even still to radical now. But I urge you people of the internet, rise up and fight against Exxon Mobil and the rest of the energy giants. Ask them, "Why was Dr. Nibbles set up? Why was Nib Nib's work destroyed?". Demand the truth, for it shall set us all free.

About Fry

My name is Fry and I'm a sneaky, orange bastard. I am deeply fascinated by water, ice cubes, and dirt. I also enjoy leaves, but I get yelled at when I eat them. If they didn't want to be eaten why'd they make them so tasty? So, I've also attempted to try human food, but I usually get spritzed with water, and then I have to clean myself off. It really just turns into a whole thing. So I've learned that when they're not paying attention I can sneak a few bites. I tried a potato once. It was magic.

I like to hang out in windows and chirp at the squirrels. All of my attempts to communicate with them had failed. I thought that if I could just make it past the force field and into the outside I could gain access to their secrets. However, even after finally making it onto the patio and squeaking at them, they did not come down from the branches. They just stood there. Laughing at me. Mocking me. Because I could not run amongst them. Whatever, at least I got to eat a spider. But sadly, it was not as good the potato.

My other hobbies include dipping my paw in cups of water, stealing hair scrunchies and hiding them under the stove, watching humans through the windows, and watching TV. I recently got into Brooklyn Nine-Nine and I'm in love with the cast. I'm also a big fan of the Office and Bob's Burgers. I've even watched every episode at least five times. I saw Futurama once. This Fry character resonated with me.

And then there's Nibbler. Oh boy, that relationship started out rough. I tried so hard to be his friend by constantly pouncing on him, bitting his tail, and licking his face. I got hissed at and smacked a few times, but we finally became cool after we decided to just talk it out. Turns out he thought I was an assassin sent by Big Oil, but that couldn't have been farther from the truth. I grew up in some bushes near the 4th street railroad tracks until I was taken and put in a tiny glass cage with colorful toys. Strange times. Regardless, now we play, cuddle, eat, and groom each other. I've learned so much about Nibbler's past, and one thing is for sure, I should definitely never, ever bring up the Sex of a Fly and alternative energy. He goes on and on forever about government conspiracies, gets mad, and ends up hiding under the bed demanding the truth until he gets hungry and forgets.
